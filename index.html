Our Policies

1. Warranty Policy

Standard Warranty: Luxury watches typically come with a 2-5 year warranty covering defects in materials or workmanship. The warranty is generally valid from the date of purchase.

Exclusions: Warranty typically doesn’t cover damage due to wear and tear, accidents, unauthorized repairs, or water damage (unless the watch is water-resistant to a certain depth).

Service Centers: Warranty services must be done at authorized service centers to maintain authenticity and preserve the warranty.

2. Return and Exchange Policy

Time Frame: Some luxury watch brands offer a 14 to 30-day return or exchange window, while others may not accept returns. However, the item must be unused and in its original condition and packaging.

Conditions: Returns and exchanges might only be allowed for defective products. Certain brands have a strict no-return policy unless the watch is faulty or damaged.

Restocking Fees: Certain brands may impose a restocking fee on returns of luxury watches, especially after the watch has been sized or engraved.

3. Authenticity and Certification

Authenticity Certificate: Upon purchase, luxury watches usually come with a certificate of authenticity, guaranteeing the watch is genuine.

Serial Number: Each luxury watch will have a unique serial number that can be used to verify its authenticity. Brands like Rolex, Patek Philippe, and Omega have their own verification systems.

Authorized Dealers: Always buy from authorized dealers to ensure the watch is genuine and comes with full brand support.

4. Pricing and Payment Policy

Retail Price: Luxury watch brands usually set a recommended retail price (RRP), and any authorized dealer should adhere to it. However, the price may vary depending on taxes, import duties, and location.

Custom Duties and Taxes: When purchasing luxury watches in India, be aware of import duties (typically 30-35% on luxury items), GST, and other taxes that could increase the final price.

Financing Options: Some luxury brands may offer financing or EMI options, especially for high-value items, making it easier for customers to purchase watches over time.

5. Customization Policy

Engraving and Personalization: Many luxury watch brands offer engraving services for personalization, often at an additional cost.

Customization Limits: Customizations are usually limited to the watch face, strap materials, and engraving. However, extensive custom modifications are often not allowed under the brand’s guidelines to maintain brand identity and value.

6. Repair and Maintenance Policy

Authorized Service Centers: For any repairs or servicing, it’s essential to visit authorized service centers. Using non-authorized services can void the warranty.

Servicing Frequency: Luxury watches should ideally be serviced every 3-5 years, depending on the brand and type (e.g., mechanical vs. quartz).

Parts Replacement: Original parts should be used for repairs and servicing to maintain the watch's integrity and value.

7. Security and Insurance Policy

Insurance: Some luxury watch owners choose to insure their timepieces, especially high-value pieces. Insurance covers theft, loss, or accidental damage.

Storage and Care: Luxury watches should be stored in safe places (preferably in a watch box with proper humidity and temperature control). Watch winders are also used for automatic watches to keep them running smoothly.

8. Delivery and Shipping Policy

International Shipping: Luxury watch brands often ship globally, but shipping can be expensive due to insurance costs, especially for high-value watches.

Packaging: Watches will be shipped with premium packaging, including branded boxes and certificates, to preserve their value.

Delivery Timelines: Delivery timelines for luxury watches, especially if they are customized or limited edition, can take several weeks or even months.

9. Customer Support Policy

After-Sales Service: Brands like Rolex, Patek Philippe, and others have dedicated customer service teams for handling inquiries, complaints, or servicing.

Online Support: Many luxury watch brands now offer online chat support or phone support to answer customer queries regarding their purchases, warranties, or services.

International Support: For customers who travel, many luxury watch brands offer international support, meaning your warranty is valid across different countries.

10. Resale Policy (Secondary Market)

Authorized Resale: Some luxury brands, such as Rolex, do not officially support the resale of their watches. However, watches from reputable sources like authorized retailers or certified pre-owned sellers maintain their authenticity.

Value Retention: Luxury watches can often appreciate in value, particularly limited-edition models or vintage pieces. Customers may choose to sell or trade their watches, often through established auction houses or secondary market dealers.

Pre-Owned Watches: Many luxury brands have started offering pre-owned watches with certified authenticity and warranty, ensuring that buyers get reliable pieces even in the secondary market.
